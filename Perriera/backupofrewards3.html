K-12 EDUCATOR OF THE YEAR HONOREE

Kindergarten Teacher, Retired
Renbrook

Steven T. Arnold

In many ways, Steve Arnold is the consummate early years teacher – patient and positive – attentive to the building blocks of learning. He inspires his students to be curious, explore the natural world around them, and embrace the best of what’s new. In other ways, Steve breaks the mold. He bikes barefoot to school each morning, constructs worlds of engineering and imagination in and outside the classroom, and he crafts his own way of delivering our core curriculum, always through the eyes of his 5 and 6-year-old students. Generations of Renbrook studentsowe their love of learning and foundational academic skillset to his extraordinary work.

It was his response to the challenges of the pandemic that brought out the best in Steve’s approach. One might say that he spent 35 years preparing for this moment. The Renbrook trails that he created and tended to over the years became his classroom; his creative and flexible mindset allowed him to pivot around the needs of his students; and hisunrivaled work ethic allowed him to deliver on the considerable extra planning and communication required. Steve excelled precisely when his students needed him most.

Renbrook’s deeply engrained commitment to outdoor teaching and learning is due in no small part to Steve’s efforts. Ed Donahue, Steve’s longtime colleague in the ELC, reflects, “During his 35 years at Renbrook, Steve has literally blazed trails to help foster a deeper appreciation and enjoyment of the natural world here at Renbrook for students, colleagues, and parents.” Steve’s legacy will live on in our lasting dedication to bringing outdoor learning to life.


HIGHER EDUCATION HONOREE

Professor of Mathematics
Manchester Community College

Diane C. Hillyer

Diane Hillyer is a Professor of Mathematics at Manchester Community College. She brings a lively and energetic presence to her classroom and in her thirty-five years as a mathematics educator, she strives to be a positive role model for her female students. She holds a master’s degree in mathematics and a bachelor’s degree in accounting. Her STEM mission driven efforts include having her students gain an appreciation for the beauty and rigor of mathematics. Helping students realize that they are capable of grasping and applying difficult, abstract topics, growing in confidence in their academic abilities, and successfully attaining academic and personal goals, is the most rewarding part of her career. Her mantra is, find your passion and do what you love. She is the recipient of the Excellence in Teaching Award granted by the Alumni Association at Manchester Community College. Diane resides in Old Saybrook with her husband Clay, is the mother of two adult sons, and is a nana for her two beloved grandchildren, Tre and Kiki.


NON-PROFIT ORGANIZATION HONOREE

4-H FIRST Robotics Team #3182

Athena’s Warriors

Athena’s Warriors 4-H FIRST Robotics Team #3182 is a non-profit community FIRST Robotics Competition team of high-school students from the Greater Hartford area of Connecticut, working out of MakerspaceCT in downtown Hartford.

FIRST has a mission to inspire young people to be science and technology leaders and innovators. Since 2010, as one of the only community-based teams in all of Connecticut, Athena’s Warriors has served as an outlet for students near Hartford to explore STEM education, projects and careers, while taking part in local robotics competitions, developing a business plan and participating in community outreach.

2022 was Athena's Warriors most successful year in all of our team history. We won the New England FIRST Hartford District Event, resulting in our first official "blue banner," as well as the event's Entrepreneurship Award. This qualified our team for both the New England District Championship in West Springfield, MA, and the FIRST World Championship in Houston, TX, where we competed the highest levels for the first time, demonstrated outstanding defense on the field and were recognized for our team brand identity.

Our team of aspiring high-school electrical and mechanical engineers, programmers, project managers, business and marketing leaders, and more are exposed to meaningful real-life experiences, life-long friendships and opportunities to give back to the community.


INNOVATOR HONOREE

Founder
PRIMR

David Grew MD MPH

Doctor Grew is a radiation oncologist and the founder of PRIMR. PRIMR produces simple, engaging digital patient education content and analytics at scale. PRIMR provides patient education videos for international clinical trials and also partners with St Francis Hospital to make PRIMR content available to patients in our community. Doctor Grew’s mission is to reduce patient suffering with better education. He is a graduate of Northwest Catholic, Fordham University (BS), Tulane School of Medicine (MD, MPH) and completed residency at NYU School of Medicine. He lives in West Hartford with his wife and 4 daughters.

WOMEN IN SCIENCE WINNER INFORMATION

HONOREE

Professor and Head, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering
University of Connecticut

Dr. Maria Chrysochoou

Dr. Maria Chrysochoou is a Professor and Head of the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Connecticut. Dr. Chrysochoou’s general research area is environmental geochemistry, with a focus on site remediation, characterization and reuse of industrial waste and construction materials. Dr. Chrysochoou serves as the Director of the EPA Region 1 Technical Assistance for Brownfields Program, serving New England communities to promote brownfield redevelopment and environmental justice. She is also the Principal Investigator of the project “Beyond Accommodation: Leveraging Neurodiversity for Engineering Innovation”, sponsored by the NSF’s Revolutionizing Engineering Department program, empowering neurodiverse students to purse an engineering education and career. As the first female department head in the School of Engineering at UCONN, Dr. Chrysochoou is also dedicated to empowering young women and underrepresented minorities to find their voice and pursue their dreams.


FINALIST

Associate Director, Manufacturing Cost Modeling
Pratt & Whitney

Sarah Koenig

Sarah is the Associate Director of Manufacturing Cost Modeling at Pratt and Whitney, a division of Raytheon Technologies. In this role, she manages a team of manufacturing engineers and project managers and oversees the creation of manufacturing cost models to support partnership and supplier pricing negotiations.

She began her career in the Manufacturing Engineering Development Program at Pratt and Whitney, completing roles in manufacturing and quality. She then became a Quality Engineer Team Leader in supplier quality. Sarah next joined the Operations Leadership Program, placing out at Collins Aerospace upon completion. At Collins, she managed transitions for castings and machined parts into Southeast Asia. She was later promoted to Castings and Machining Lead, gaining responsibility for the team managing China and India.

Sarah subsequently returned to Pratt and Whitney as an Industrial Manager, responsible for mitigating Operations-related risks. Sarah was then promoted to Deputy Production Chief. Here she spearheaded the industrialization of key configuration changes for the Next Generation Product Family, simultaneously leading several cross-functional teams comprised of engineering, manufacturing, supply chain, and quality.

Sarah is a Life Member of the Society of Women Engineers (SWE) and has held leadership roles at the section, region, and Society level. She also serves on the Board of Directors for Camp Mujigae, a Korean heritage camp.

She graduated from the International Engineering Program at the University of Rhode Island, earning a B.S. in Industrial Engineering and a B.A. in French, in addition to an MBA from Carnegie Mellon University.


FINALIST

Secondary Science Teacher
Stamford Public Schools

Danielle Weber

Danielle Weber has been teaching high school science for 16 years and is currently working at the Academy of Information Technology and Engineering in Stamford, CT. She earned a BS from Elon University in Biology and Secondary Science Education, an M. Ed. from SUNY Buffalo in Science and the Public, and an M. Ed. from Sacred Heart University in Educational Leadership.

At AITE, she is the faculty advisor for the Science National Honor Society, Red Cross Club, and RSPACEE, an afterschool program in which high schoolers share their love of science with elementary and middle school students. Danielle is a TEAM Mentor and Reflection Paper Reviewer; as such, she has had the joy of working with countless new and aspiring science teachers. She is AITE’s science department head representative and the lead teacher for science curriculum for Stamford Public Schools.

Danielle has been working with Project Lead The Way for 11 years and was named the 2021-2022 National Biomed Teacher of the Year. She is a Master Teacher for both Human Body Systems and Medical Interventions. In this role, Danielle is a mentor and instructor for teachers new to PLTW courses through in-person and online professional development. Danielle also shares her love of mentoring through her work as a coach and presenter for Mass STEM Hub, an organization focused on enhancing STEM opportunities for students, teachers, and schools throughout Massachusetts.

Outside of the classroom, Danielle enjoys spending time with her husband, Jeff, and their two boys, Joshua and Donovan.
